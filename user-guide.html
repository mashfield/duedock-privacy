What is DueDock?

DueDock is a simple app that helps you see exactly which bills are due and when they're due. It shows you your upcoming bills and income so you can plan ahead and avoid missing payments.

The main goal: Help you organize your bills and income in one place.

Smart Features

ğŸ¤– Automatic Calculations


DueDock does all the math for you:
- Figures out which bills are due when
- Calculates totals automatically
- Updates as you pay bills

ğŸ“± Same-Day Conflicts


Sometimes bills are due on the same day as your income. DueDock shows these as conflicts because sometimes pre-authorized payments require funds to be in your account the day before the payment is actually withdrawn.

This creates uncertainty: if you pay a bill on the same day you receive income, the payment might not clear in time for the withdrawal.

DueDock shows these as conflicts and lets you decide which to include in totals. Use a toggle to include pre-authorized payments when you're confident the timing will work.

ğŸ”„ Variable Bills


Some bills change each month (like credit cards):
- Mark bills as "variable"
- Enter actual amount when you get the bill
- DueDock uses the real amount for calculations

ğŸ’° Payment History

Common Questions

Q: How is DueDock different from other budget apps?
A: DueDock focuses on upcoming bills and income rather than tracking past spending. It's designed to help you plan ahead.

Q: What if I have irregular income?
A: DueDock handles different pay frequencies (weekly, bi-weekly, monthly). Just enter when you expect to get paid.

Q: Is my financial information secure?
A: Yes! DueDock stores everything on your device only. No cloud storage, no data sharing, complete privacy.

Q: Can I use DueDock for business expenses?
A: DueDock is designed for personal finances, but many users track small business expenses alongside personal ones.

Q: What if I miss a bill?
A: DueDock will show it in red as overdue. You can still mark it as paid and the app will update your calculations.
