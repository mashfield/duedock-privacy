DueDock Calculations Guide

Understanding DueDock's calculations in plain language. This guide explains how DueDock handles your financial calculations, from simple bill tracking to complex payment scenarios.

Core Concepts

Balance Calculations

Available Balance = Account Balance - Total Due Bills

DueDock calculates your available balance by subtracting all unpaid bills from your account balance:

Example:
Account Balance: $2,500
Due Bills: $800 (rent) + $150 (utilities) + $50 (phone) = $1,000
Available Balance: $2,500 - $1,000 = $1,500

Coverage Check Logic

The Coverage Check shows bills due before your next income source:

Step 1: Find your next income date
Step 2: Include all bills with due dates ≤ income date
Step 3: Calculate total of those bills
Step 4: Compare to current balance
Example:
Today: June 15
Next Income: June 30 (paycheck $2,000)
Bills before June 30: $800 (June 20) + $150 (June 25) = $950
Coverage Check: Can you pay $950 with current balance?

Bill Types & Calculations

Fixed Bills

Fixed bills have the same amount each cycle:

Total Due = Bill Amount
Examples:
• Rent: $1,200/month
• Car Payment: $350/month
• Phone Bill: $50/month

Variable Bills

Variable bills change each cycle. DueDock tracks both expected and actual amounts:

Expected Amount = Your estimate
Actual Amount = Real bill amount
Difference = Actual - Expected
Example (Credit Card):
Expected: $200
Actual: $285
Difference: +$85 (you spent $85 more than expected)
Best Practice: Update variable bills as soon as you receive the actual amount to maintain accurate coverage calculations.

Recurring Payment Calculations

Frequency Handling

DueDock automatically advances due dates based on frequency:

Monthly: Same day next month
Weekly: Same day next week
Bi-weekly: Same day in 2 weeks
Quarterly: Same day in 3 months
Yearly: Same day next year
Example:
Bill due: June 15, 2024
Frequency: Monthly
Next due date: July 15, 2024

End-of-Month Handling

Important: DueDock handles month-end dates intelligently to avoid invalid dates.
Example:
Bill due: January 31
Frequency: Monthly
Next due: February 28 (or 29 in leap year)
Following: March 31 (returns to 31st)

Payment Scenarios

Partial Payments

When you pay part of a bill, DueDock tracks the remaining balance:

Remaining Balance = Bill Amount - Partial Payment
Status: Partially Paid
Example:
Bill Amount: $500
Partial Payment: $200
Remaining: $300 (still due)

Installments

Installments break large bills into equal payments:

Installment Amount = Total Bill ÷ Number of Installments
Total Paid = Installment Amount × Installments Paid
Example:
Total Bill: $1,200
Installments: 3
Each payment: $400
After 2 payments: $800 paid, $400 remaining

Planning Period Calculations

Extended Coverage

The Planning Period shows your financial picture for any date range:

Period Total = Sum of all bills in date range
Period Coverage = Current Balance - Period Total
Example:
Planning Period: June 15 - July 15
Bills in period: $1,200 (rent) + $600 (groceries) + $300 (utilities) = $2,100
Current Balance: $3,000
Coverage for period: $3,000 - $2,100 = $900

Income Integration

When you include income sources in the Planning Period:

Net Coverage = (Balance + Income) - Total Bills
Example:
Balance: $1,000
Income in period: $2,000 (paycheck)
Bills in period: $1,500
Net Coverage: ($1,000 + $2,000) - $1,500 = $1,500

Advanced Calculations

Same-Day Conflicts

DueDock identifies when multiple bills are due on the same day:

Conflict Total = Sum of bills due on same date
Conflict Risk = Conflict Total ÷ Daily Available Funds
Example:
June 15 bills: $800 (rent) + $500 (car payment) + $200 (insurance) = $1,500
Daily available: $100
Conflict Risk: $1,500 ÷ $100 = 15 days of funds needed

Variable Bill Averages

DueDock can calculate average amounts for variable bills:

Average = Sum of last 3 amounts ÷ 3
Variance = Current Amount - Average
Example (Electric Bill):
Last 3 months: $180, $220, $200
Average: ($180 + $220 + $200) ÷ 3 = $200
Current month: $250
Variance: $250 - $200 = +$50 (higher than average)

Savings & Goals

Savings Calculations

Savings goals are calculated separately from bills:

Progress = Amount Saved ÷ Goal Amount
Remaining = Goal Amount - Amount Saved
Example:
Goal: $5,000 vacation
Saved: $2,000
Progress: $2,000 ÷ $5,000 = 40%
Remaining: $3,000

Recommended Savings

Financial Health Tip: DueDock recommends saving 20% of income after essential bills.
Recommended Savings = (Income - Essential Bills) × 0.20

Edge Cases & Special Scenarios

Leap Year Handling

February 29 bills:
Non-leap year: February 28
Leap year: February 29
DueDock automatically adjusts for leap years

Weekend Adjustments

Note: DueDock does NOT automatically adjust for weekends. Bills due on weekends remain due on those dates.

Currency & Decimals

DueDock handles:
• 2 decimal places for currency
• Commas in large numbers (1,000)
• Period as decimal separator (0.50)
Maximum amount: $999,999.99

Performance Considerations

Calculation Limits

Optimal performance with:
• Up to 100 active bills
• Planning periods up to 1 year
• Up to 10 income sources
More items may slow calculations

Calculation Frequency

Real-time updates for:
• Adding/editing bills
• Changing amounts
• Docking payments
Planning Period recalculates when date changes

Troubleshooting Calculations

Common Issues

Issue: "Coverage Check shows wrong total"
Cause: Variable bills not updated with actual amounts
Fix: Update variable bills with real amounts
Issue: "Planning Period seems off"
Cause: Date range includes past bills
Fix: Set start date to today or future date
Issue: "Balance doesn't match bank"
Cause: Pending transactions not in DueDock
Fix: Add pending transactions manually

Best Practices

Accuracy Tips:
• Update variable bills immediately
• Review Coverage Check daily
• Use Planning Period for monthly budgeting
• Set realistic estimates for variable bills
Planning Tips:
• Include buffer amounts in estimates
• Plan for seasonal bill variations
• Account for annual expenses monthly
• Review calculations regularly

Summary

DueDock's calculations are designed to be transparent and accurate. Understanding these formulas helps you make better financial decisions and catch potential issues before they become problems.

Remember: DueDock is a tool to help you plan, but always verify with your actual bank statements and financial institutions.
